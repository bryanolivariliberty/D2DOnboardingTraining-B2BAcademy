Project purpose
Why we are building this now

The Door-to-Door (D2D) channel serves the SOHO segment — businesses with 1 to 10 employees — and operates with one of the highest turnover rates across all B2B sales channels at Liberty Business. This creates a recurring challenge: every time a rep leaves, the training burden falls back on a Knowledge & Training team with limited capacity, which directly impacts how quickly new reps become field-ready.

The B2B Academy solves this by making training available on demand, self-paced, and repeatable — without requiring trainer availability for every onboarding cycle. New hires can start from day one, progress through structured modules at their own pace, and reach certification readiness in approximately two weeks rather than depending on live instructor-led sessions.

We are targeting an August 2026 launch to coincide with the next planned D2D onboarding cycle. All content production is being sequenced to have the full Learning Plan published and accessible in the LMS before that date.

Program structure
How the Academy is organized

The D2D Onboarding Learning Plan consists of 11 modules delivered sequentially in the LMS. Module 1 is delivered offline (in-person, Teams, or job aid) and covers LMS orientation before access is granted. Modules 2 through 10 are self-paced in the LMS. Module 11 is the Final Certification Exam.

Each module contains one or more videos (maximum 8 minutes each), a knowledge check after every module, and a final certification exam at ≥85% pass threshold. Content is produced in both English and Spanish.

Technology stack
Content production tools

Google NotebookLM — source material is uploaded per module, generating narration scripts, on-screen cues, objection cards, and knowledge check questions in both languages.

NotebookLM Video Overview — AI-generated explainer videos (MP4) produced directly from uploaded source documents using the Customize field in the Studio panel.

Adobe Captivate — SCORM 1.2 packaging of videos with quiz layers, interactions, and branching scenarios for system simulations (VU Console LCPR, B2B Quick Sales App, Enterprise Sales Console).

LMS — hosting, sequencing, progress tracking, certification, and manager notifications.

Content Production Process Per-module workflow · NotebookLM → Video → LMS
Process context Each training module in the approved v5 agenda has a defined total time. Because the NotebookLM Video Overview tool has a maximum output of approximately 8 minutes per video, modules with more content must be divided into multiple individual videos. The total number of videos per module is determined by dividing the module's target duration by the 8-minute cap, then grouping content logically by topic. Module 2 (60 min) resulted in 8 videos of 7 minutes each.
1
Review approved agenda & define video breakdown
Training Specialist

Using the approved D2D Learning Plan v5, the training specialist reviews the module's total time allocation. Content is divided into individual videos, each with a maximum of 8 minutes. Topics are grouped logically — competitive videos together, product catalog videos together — so each video has a single clear learning objective.

~15–20 min per module
▼
2
Gather & upload source materials to NotebookLM
Training Specialist

All source materials for the module are gathered — product sheets, rate cards, SOPs, competitive battle cards, presentation decks, and policy documents. These are uploaded to a dedicated NotebookLM notebook for that module. NotebookLM ingests and indexes each file (green checkmark = ready).

  • Sources must be uploaded and fully processed before running any prompt
  • Missing source documents must be flagged to Bryan before starting
  • The quality of the output is directly tied to the completeness of the sources
~10–20 min per module
▼
3
Run Chat prompt → generate script & content blueprint
Training Specialist

The Chat prompt (English) from the NotebookLM Prompt Guide is pasted into the Chat panel (center). NotebookLM generates a full content output: narration script with scenes, on-screen text cues, objection cards (for competitive videos), reference cards, and knowledge check questions. The Spanish version is generated by running the same prompt with the Spanish instruction header prepended.

  • English prompt → English script, cues, quiz questions
  • Spanish instruction + prompt → Spanish version of all content
  • All [VERIFY FROM SOURCE] flags must be checked against uploaded documents before proceeding
  • Output saved as: M0X_VX_[Title]_EN_DRAFT and _ES_DRAFT
~20 min per video (EN + ES)
▼
4
Review & verify Chat output
Training Specialist + Bryan Olivari

The Chat draft is reviewed against the uploaded source documents. Every [VERIFY FROM SOURCE] flag is confirmed or corrected. This step exists to ensure accuracy before the video is generated — once the video is produced, corrections require a full regeneration.

  • Factual accuracy checked (plan names, pricing, coverage, platform distinctions)
  • Tone reviewed (competitive videos must not disparage competitors directly)
  • Spanish version reviewed for natural Puerto Rican professional register
Review before generating video
▼
5
Generate Video Overview (English then Spanish)
Training Specialist

In the Studio panel (right side of NotebookLM), click Video Overview. Paste the Video Overview prompt into the Describe your own / Customize field — this is a 2–4 sentence direction that tells NotebookLM what topic, audience, and tone to apply. The field has a short character limit; the approved prompt guide includes the exact text for each video.

  • English prompt → English MP4 video
  • Spanish prompt → Spanish MP4 video (re-run with Spanish version of the customize prompt)
  • Branding instruction included in each prompt: orange and blue brand only, Logo LB RGB, knowledge check reminder at the opening
  • Videos are generated from the uploaded sources — not from the Chat script
~20–30 min per video generation First export usually needs review
▼
6
Review video & correct errors
Training Specialist + Bryan Olivari

The generated MP4 is reviewed against the verified Chat script. The first export of each video typically requires correction — the AI may over-explain a concept, include an inaccurate detail not caught by the source documents, or present information in an order that does not match the approved scene structure.

  • If correction is minor: adjust the Customize prompt and regenerate
  • If correction is structural: update source documents and regenerate
  • Spanish video reviewed separately for language accuracy and cultural register
Most first exports need at least one correction round
▼
7
Package in Adobe Captivate & publish SCORM
Training Specialist

The approved MP4 is imported into Adobe Captivate as a video slide. Knowledge check questions from the Chat output are added as quiz slides. The project is published as a SCORM 1.2 package and uploaded to the LMS.

  • Quiz questions: multiple choice, 4 options, ≥80% pass threshold per module
  • Final Certification Exam (M11): 30 questions, ≥85%, 1 attempt, LMS native quiz engine
  • Certificate auto-issued by LMS on pass; manager email notification triggered
▼
8
Create course record in LMS & publish
Bryan Olivari

Each video is published as an individual course in the B2B Academy. The course record includes a Brief Description (≤140 chars, for the course card) and a Detailed Overview (≤8,000 chars, for the course page) in both English and Spanish. The SCORM package is attached, sequencing rules are set, and the course is added to the D2D Learning Plan.

⚠ Key constraint: 8-minute maximum per video NotebookLM Video Overview generates videos of approximately 5–8 minutes depending on the source depth and topic scope. Modules with more content (e.g., Module 2 at 60 min, Module 6 at 60 min) must be divided into multiple individual videos before production begins. The division is planned during Step 1 of the process and documented in the material tab.
